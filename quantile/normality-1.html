Test the normality of a variable in Stata

In Stata, we can test normality of a distribution by either graphical or numerical methods.

  1. Graphical methods include drawing a
    1. stem-and-leaf plot,
    2. scatterplot,
    3. box-plot,
    4. histogram,
    5. probability-probability (P-P) plot,
    6. or quantile-quantile (Q-Q) plot.
  2. Numerical methods involve computing the
    1. Shapiro-Wilk test,
    2. Shapiro-Francia test,
    3. and Skewness/Kurtosis tests.

Be aware that in these tests, the null hypothesis states that the variable is normally distributed.

The examples below are for the variable score:
